## Notes for when this becomes a real repo

- **Pick a licence deliberately.** Options:
  - **Proprietary / "all rights reserved"** — best while it's a commercial idea you want to control (default above).
  - **Source-available** (e.g. a non-commercial or "fair source" licence) — share the code, restrict commercial use.
  - **Permissive open source** (e.g. MIT) — only if you intend the engine to be freely reusable.
  - Consider **splitting**: open-source the generic *engine*, keep *content* (question banks) and *brand* proprietary.
